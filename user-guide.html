Getting started

Your first-time checklist before creating any document.

  1. Optional first step: Click Login / Sign up on Home to create a secure account (email + password). This protects your data, but it's optional.
  2. Open Home from the left sidebar — this is your main dashboard.
  3. Tap the person icon (top) or go to Settings → Personal Information to add your company name, GSTIN, address, and logo.
  4. Open Menu to see the demo food catalog, or Inventory to add products you sell (name, rate, HSN, tax).
  5. Pick a document type from Quick Actions (Invoice, Delivery Challan, Quotation, etc.).
  6. Fill the form, choose a layout/format, then tap Preview Document.
  7. On the preview page use Download / Share or Print to save or send the PDF.
  8. You'll see notifications (toasts) when actions complete — they're helpful confirmations.
  9. Back up regularly: Go to Settings → Your Data → click Download to back up all your data as a JSON file you own.
  10. Not ready to enter real data? Open the test documents section — four TEST-* samples share one product (2 × ₹350), ₹10 delivery, and GST (invoice total ₹837.80 ≈ $10).

Creating a tax invoice

GST invoices with items, tax, signature, and stamp.

  1. From Home, tap New Invoice or open Invoice Layouts and pick Format 1, 2, or 3.
  2. On the editor (GST Invoice Generator), use tabs: Party Details for company & client, Transaction Details for invoice lines and totals.
  3. Tap Add Company / Add Client to enter details, or load saved profiles from the dropdown.
  4. Under Invoice Info, set invoice number, date, due date, and place of supply.
  5. Add products (name, qty, rate, HSN, tax %). Use + for more rows.
  6. Optional: Taxes, Payment instructions, Shipping charges, Watermark, Stamp.
  7. Draw or load a signature in the signature pad section.
  8. Tax calculation now rounds consistently to two decimals and GST types automatically split into CGST + SGST.
  9. Select format at the bottom and click Preview Document.

Tip: Use Document History on the editor to reopen past invoices without retyping.

Convert document

Reuse data from one document type in another.

  1. Home → Convert Document.
  2. Pick a saved document from the list (must exist in history).
  3. Choose the target type — e.g. Quotation → Invoice.
  4. Confirm; the editor opens with fields pre-filled. Review totals and preview.

Tip: Create at least one invoice or quotation first so history has something to convert.

Document preview, PDF & print

What you see after tapping Preview Document.

  1. The preview opens in a new tab with your chosen format layout.
  2. Use ← Back (top-left) to return without losing data in the editor.
  3. Download / Share — save PDF or share via apps on your device.
  4. Print (Vector PDF) — best quality for professional printing.
  5. Edit Invoice — jumps back to the editor with your data.
  6. Watermark and stamp appear on the document if you enabled them in the editor.
  7. Screenshot protection is active on the app page when supported: browser screenshot and copy shortcuts are blocked, and a temporary overlay is shown.
  8. Note: OS-level screen capture tools may still capture the page, so use PDF export for the safest copy.

Notifications 🔔

Stay informed with in-app notifications for actions and reminders.

What are notifications?

Notifications appear as brief messages (toasts) when you perform actions in SmartBiz, like creating invoices, saving data, or when backups are due. They're stored locally and never sent anywhere.

Where do notifications appear?

  • Toast notifications — appear at the top-right of your screen automatically and fade after 5 seconds (or stay if you need more time).
  • Bell icon — on the Home page header, showing count of unread notifications.
  • Notification history — accessible from Settings → Notifications to review all notifications.

How to manage notifications

  1. Go to Settings → Notifications.
  2. Toggle Enable Notifications on/off (default: on).
  3. Click Send Test Notification to see how they work.
  4. View your Notification History to see past messages.
  5. Click Clear All to remove old notifications (they're not deleted from your backup).

Tip: Notifications are saved locally on your device. Clearing them doesn't affect your data — it just removes the notification messages.

Recurring invoices

Set up automatic invoice schedules and reminders from the recurring dashboard.

How recurring invoices work

Recurring invoices are automatically generated from your schedule each time the next run date arrives. You can create a schedule, choose a frequency, add an optional recipient email, and pause or delete it anytime.

  1. Go to the Recurring page from the menu or Home quick actions.
  2. Click Create schedule and fill in the title, client, amount, frequency, interval, and start date.
  3. Optionally add a recipient email and reminder days before the next run.
  4. Click Create schedule to save it. It appears in the schedule list immediately.
  5. The system calculates the next run date using your frequency and interval.
  6. The schedule remains active until you click Pause or delete it.

Tips for using recurring invoices

  • Use a clear title so you can identify each schedule quickly in the list.
  • Keep recipient email optional unless you want reminders sent directly.
  • Pause instead of deleting when you want to stop a schedule temporarily.
  • Check next run dates at the top of the Recurring page to stay on schedule.

Backup & data management 💾

Download your complete data as a backup file that you own and control.

What's included in a backup?

Your backup includes everything: invoices, clients, products, settings, notifications, and more — all in a single JSON file.

  • All invoices and documents
  • Client and product information
  • Your settings and preferences
  • Signatures and stamps
  • Catalog / menu data
  • Auto-save settings

How to download a backup

  1. Open Settings from the sidebar.
  2. Go to Your Data section.
  3. You'll see a summary: "12 data items stored — 2.5 MB".
  4. Click the blue Download button.
  5. A file called smarbiz-backup-2026-05-27.json downloads to your device's Downloads folder.
  6. Save it somewhere safe — this is your complete data backup.

How to restore from backup

  1. Open Settings → Your Data.
  2. Click the orange Restore button.
  3. Select a backup file you downloaded previously.
  4. You'll be asked to confirm: "This will replace all your current data."
  5. Click Confirm — your data is restored instantly.
  6. A success notification appears when complete.

Backup file information

DetailInformation
FormatJSON (human-readable text format)
File namesmarbiz-backup-YYYY-MM-DD.json (e.g., smarbiz-backup-2026-05-27.json)
LocationYour device's Downloads folder (you control it)

SizeVaries (typically 1-5 MB depending on data)

Backup best practices

  • Back up weekly — click Download once a week to have fresh backups.
  • Keep multiple copies — save backups on your computer and perhaps external storage.
  • Before major changes — always download a backup before trying new features.
  • Share safely — backup files are JSON text; only share with trusted people or systems.
  • Device migration — backup on your old device, restore on your new one to keep all data.

Clearing temporary data

Sometimes your browser stores temporary files. You can clear them without losing important data:

  1. Settings → Your Data → click Clear Temporary Data.
  2. This removes cache files but keeps invoices, clients, and settings.
  3. If something breaks, you can restore from a backup file anytime.

Important: Your backup is yours. You own the file, can share it, store it wherever you want, and restore it anytime. No external service, no subscriptions — complete control.

Signature & round stamp

Authorize documents like a physical stamp.

  1. In the invoice editor, scroll to Signature — draw on the pad or load a saved signature.
  2. Tap Save Signature; use Manage to pick from the gallery.
  3. For the round seal: tap Add Stamp, enter top ring text (company), bottom text (e.g. OFFICIAL), center city.
  4. Choose stamp colour and save; it overlays the signature area on preview.
  5. Adjust stamp size in settings if the app stores a size preference.

Tip: On preview pages only one Back button appears (top-left) — use it to return to the editor.

Document history

Find and reopen past work.

  1. On the invoice editor, tap Document History.
  2. Browse by client name, invoice number, or date.
  3. Open a row to reload that document into the editor or preview.
  4. History is stored on the server and in your browser; refresh Home to see the latest list.

CMS editor

Manage pages, SEO metadata, scheduling, and drafts from one central dashboard.

  1. Open CMS from the Home menu or visit /cms.html.
  2. Enter the private CMS password to unlock the dashboard.
  3. Use the CMS toolbar to search pages, filter by type, and filter by status (Published, Draft, Scheduled).
  4. Click New Page to create a page, or use Edit, Duplicate, and Delete on existing pages.
  5. In the editor, fill page details including Slug, Title, Meta description, Meta title, Keywords, and Canonical URL.
  6. Select a Page type (Custom, Landing, Blog, FAQ, Product) to organize your content better.
  7. Use the Schedule publishing toggle to set a publish date and optional unpublish date.
  8. Press Preview to open the page in a new tab and verify the live content and SEO metadata.
  9. The editor auto-saves drafts every 20 seconds when editing an existing page.
  10. Use Page versions to view history and revert to a previous save if needed.
  11. Export pages as JSON for backups, or import JSON pages into the CMS dashboard.

Tip: Only published pages are served on the site. Scheduled pages stay hidden until the publish time, and pages with an unpublish date expire automatically.

Login, signup & account 🔐

Secure sign-in to protect your data and keep your session active.

Why sign up?

  • Protection — only you can access your data with email & password.
  • Email verification — confirms your email is real.
  • Session persistence — your settings stay with your account.
  • Remember me — option to stay logged in longer on your device.

How to sign up

  1. Click Login / Sign up on Home or the top header.
  2. On the login page, click Create a new account.
  3. Enter your email and password (at least 8 characters recommended).
  4. Confirm your password.
  5. Click Sign up — an email verification link appears.
  6. Check your browser console (F12 → Console tab) for the verification link.
  7. Click the link to verify your email.
  8. Return to login and sign in with your email and password.

How to sign in

  1. Click Login / Sign up on Home or the header.
  2. Enter your email and password.
  3. Optional: check Remember me to stay logged in for one week (default: 2 hours).
  4. Click Sign in or press Enter.
  5. After login, you'll see your email in the header with a logout option.

Forgot your password?

  1. On the login page, click Forgot password?.
  2. Enter your email address and click Send reset link.
  3. A reset link appears in the browser console (F12 → Console).
  4. Click the link or copy it into the address bar.
  5. Set your new password (8+ characters) and click Reset password.
  6. Return to login and sign in with your new password.

How to log out

  • Look for your email in the header (top-right area).
  • Click the logout icon (usually an arrow or × symbol next to your email).
  • You'll be logged out and returned to the login page.

Security tips

  • Use a strong password — mix letters, numbers, and symbols (not just your name).
  • Don't share your password — SmartBiz support will never ask for it.
  • Log out on shared devices — always click logout when using someone else's computer.
  • Check "Remember me" carefully — only on your personal device if you want the extended session.

Note: Email verification and password reset links appear in the browser console. This is for development/local use. In production, you would receive real emails.
